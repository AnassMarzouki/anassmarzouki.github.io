Morocco Earthquake Model

The Morocco Earthquake Model is a national scientific initiative dedicated to building the first comprehensive seismic hazard and risk model for the country. Launched through a collaboration between the Fonds de Solidarité contre les Événements Catastrophiques (FSEC), Atlantic Re, and the Global Earthquake Model (GEM) Foundation, this project represents a major step toward understanding and mitigating Morocco’s seismic risks. As part of the scientific team, I contribute to advancing the research and technical components that underpin this national effort.

The project’s development began with a three-day workshop in Casablanca, bringing together Moroccan experts and GEM’s international specialists to align on scientific goals and technical methodologies. Discussions focused on the current state of seismic research in Morocco, identifying key data gaps, research priorities, and areas for collaboration. Through knowledge exchange on source modeling, ground motion characterization, exposure, and vulnerability, the team set the foundation for a robust, transparent, and data-driven national model.

At the core of this effort lies OpenQuake Engine, GEM’s open-source platform for earthquake hazard and risk assessment. This tool enables scientists and engineers to model complex seismic scenarios, simulate ground shaking, and estimate potential losses across Morocco’s diverse geologies and built environments. Training sessions during the workshop ensured that Moroccan researchers and institutions can independently develop, refine, and apply the model to support decision-making.

Ultimately, the Morocco Earthquake Model is more than a scientific exercise—it is a partnership between international expertise and local knowledge, designed to enhance Morocco’s resilience to future earthquakes. By combining cutting-edge research with local data and institutional collaboration, this project aims to provide the scientific foundation for safer cities, informed urban planning, and effective disaster preparedness across the country.
