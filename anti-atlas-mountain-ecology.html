Moroccan Anti-Atlas Mountain Ecology

The Moroccan Anti-Atlas Mountain Ecology project is a joint research initiative between the Université Internationale de Rabat (UIR) and the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). The project brings together an interdisciplinary team of archaeologists, anthropologists, and geographers from Morocco and the United States to study the unique agricultural terrace systems of the Anti-Atlas Mountains — an extraordinary landscape shaped by centuries of human adaptation and ecological balance. For hundreds of years, local communities in the Anti-Atlas have engineered and maintained intricate networks of stone terraces, allowing sustainable agriculture to thrive in a challenging semi-arid environment. These terraces not only reflect the region’s deep cultural and ecological heritage but also offer invaluable lessons in resilience, water management, and landscape conservation. The project seeks to document how these systems were built, how they endured through time, and what factors have contributed to their preservation. By combining archaeological fieldwork, geospatial mapping, and ethnographic research, the team aims to build a comprehensive understanding of the environmental, social, and historical dimensions that sustain terrace agriculture. The research also integrates modern tools such as remote sensing, GIS analysis, and ecological surveys to assess current challenges posed by climate change, depopulation, and land-use shifts. Ultimately, the Moroccan Anti-Atlas Mountain Ecology project is about safeguarding a living heritage. By blending traditional knowledge with modern scientific approaches, the collaboration between UIR and UCLA seeks to promote sustainable strategies for preserving these remarkable landscapes — ensuring that the wisdom of past generations continues to guide future ones in caring for Morocco’s mountain ecosystems.
